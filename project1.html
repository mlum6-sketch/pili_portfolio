Visions of Leadership

A text-based portrait series honoring Hawaiian leaders through mele, speeches, and archival documents.

Each portrait is built entirely from meaningful text — words spoken or written by the leader themselves. This style honors ʻike kupuna and validates Hawaiian storytelling as art, language, and identity.

Changing to this style mid-semester was risky but transformative. Once I shifted, everything flowed. I finally felt aligned with my purpose and with these historical figures.

Process

  • Medium: Digital word-art illustration
  • Tools: Illustrator, Photoshop
  • Themes: Sovereignty, identity, moʻolelo
